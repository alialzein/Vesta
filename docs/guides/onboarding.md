# First-run setup — teaching Vesta about you

The very first time you sign in, Vesta shows a short, friendly **welcome wizard**.
Its goal is to learn a few things about how you work so the dashboard feels personal
from day one. It takes under a minute, and **you can skip it** at any point.

## What it asks

1. **Your role** — what you do, so Vesta frames your work appropriately.
2. **Preferred tone** — how you like replies and summaries to sound (e.g. concise,
   warm, formal).
3. **VIPs** — people whose email should always reach you.
4. **Topics** — subjects that matter to you.
5. **Connect your mailbox** — a pointer to link Outlook (you can do this now or
   later in Settings).

## What Vesta does with your answers

- Your answers are saved as your own **memories and preferences** — Vesta's
  starting knowledge about you. Nothing is shared with anyone else.
- **Nothing is acted on automatically.** At this stage Vesta is only *remembering*
  what you told it. (AI suggestions and drafts come later, and always with your
  approval.)
- The **VIPs** you list feed directly into email filtering — VIP senders are always
  shown and never hidden as noise (see [How Vesta filters your email](email-filtering.md)).

## Skipping or revisiting

- **Skip** sends you straight to the dashboard; you can set everything up later in
  Settings.
- Once you've finished or skipped, Vesta won't show the wizard again — returning
  visits go straight to your dashboard.
