# Daily Brief & Focus Mode — start the day already oriented

Every morning, Vesta writes you a short brief about **your own queue** — what
actually needs you today and the one item to start with, with the reason. Then
**Focus Mode (Clear My Day)** walks you through the queue one item at a time
until it's empty. No internet news, no fluff — just your work, ordered.

## Where to find it

Both live on the **Today** dashboard:

- The **Morning Brief card** at the top — the headline, the brief, and a
  **"Start here"** suggestion.
- The **Clear My Day** button on that same card — opens Focus Mode full-screen.

## The morning brief

- **First open of the day:** the card shows the standard summary immediately,
  with a small "Vesta is writing today's brief…" line. A few seconds later the
  AI brief **writes itself in** word by word — written from your open items
  (who's waiting, what's overdue, what can wait).
- **The numbers under the brief are always live.** The small "5 open ·
  1 overdue · 2 waiting on you" line is computed from what's on your radar
  **right now** — mark something done and it updates instantly. It never comes
  from the AI's (cached) words, so it can't be out of date.
- **The brief can't lie to you.** The AI text is written once and cached — but
  if your queue changes in a way that would make it wrong (something becomes
  overdue after it was written, or its "start here" item is gone), Vesta
  detects the mismatch, keeps the standard brief, and quietly rewrites today's
  brief for the real queue. The AI is also forbidden from stating counts or
  "nothing is overdue" claims — the live numbers line owns the facts.
- **"Start here" is THE recommendation.** It names the one item Vesta would
  tackle first (overdue beats waiting-people beats score) with the reason in
  plain words — and the dashboard now **opens with that item already
  selected**, so the AI rail's "Next Best Action" is about the same item. One
  voice, never two competing "do this first". Hover the Start-here row and a
  **glowing thread** draws from the brief to that exact card on the radar —
  Vesta literally points at it (click = same jump as before).
- **One AI call per day.** The brief is written once each morning and cached —
  reloading the page, or opening Vesta on your other laptop, shows the same
  brief without paying again. It refreshes after **your** midnight (Settings →
  Timezone), so a new day means a new brief wherever you are. (A stale-queue
  rewrite, when needed, is one extra small call.)
- **It never blocks your morning.** If AI is off, paused, or over its budget,
  you simply keep the standard brief — the dashboard works exactly as before.

## Focus Mode — Clear My Day

Click **Clear My Day** and the screen clears to a single card:

- **One item at a time**, highest priority first (the brief's "start here" pick
  goes first when there is one). You see who it's from, the summary, the
  deadline, and the one suggested next step.
- **Act or move on:**
  - **Mark done** — clears it (it returns if the sender replies again).
  - **Draft reply / Draft follow-up** — opens the same composer you already
    know; approve & send, and Focus Mode continues.
  - **Tomorrow** — snoozes it until tomorrow 9 AM.
  - **Skip** — moves on without judging; skipped items are offered again at
    the end ("Go through skips").
- The **progress bar** fills as you go — "4 of 9 handled" — until the
  **"Day cleared."** screen.
- **Esc** (or the ✕) exits at any time. Nothing is lost: everything you didn't
  handle is still on the radar.

## Quick start

1. Open the dashboard in the morning → read the headline and the brief.
2. Click **Start here** → handle the one item Vesta picked.
3. Click **Clear My Day** → work the rest top-down: Done / Draft / Tomorrow /
   Skip.
4. When you see **"Day cleared."**, you're genuinely done — anything new will
   land on the radar as mail syncs.

## Good to know

- The brief reads only **your open items** (titles, senders, deadlines,
  summaries) — nothing else, and nothing is invented: if the queue is empty,
  the brief says so.
- Sending still requires your explicit **Approve & Send** — Focus Mode never
  sends anything by itself.
- Cost shows up in the admin AI Control Center under the **brief** feature
  (one small call per user per day).
