# Memory & Rules — teach Vesta once, it applies it everywhere

Memory & Rules is where you teach Vesta how you work: who matters, how you like
replies written, what to hand off, and what it must never do. Everything you
save here is used **every time** Vesta ranks your radar and writes a draft.

Open it from the sidebar: **Intelligence → Memory & Rules**.

## What you can teach Vesta

| Type | Example | What it changes |
|------|---------|-----------------|
| **VIP person** | "Treat maya@cedars.com as VIP" | Their mail always passes the noise filter, scores higher on the radar, and the AI is told the sender is a VIP |
| **Tone** | "Short sentences, no fluff" | Every draft is written that way |
| **Delegation rule** | "Invoices go to Lina" | The AI suggests handing matching items to that person |
| **Do NOT do** | "Never promise same-day delivery" | A hard limit — drafts will not cross it |
| **Project context** | "Q3 launch is the top priority" | The AI uses it when ranking and explaining items |
| **Company context** | "We are a 40-person logistics firm" | Background the AI can rely on instead of guessing |
| **Preference** | "Always answer the CEO same-day" | General guidance for ranking and replies |

Type the memory, pick its type, and press **Remember this**. Your own memories
are active immediately.

### The VIP shortcut

If a VIP memory contains an **email address**, Vesta also marks that sender as
VIP in its address book. That means their messages are never hidden by the
noise filter, they get a priority boost on the radar, and the AI sees "this
sender is a VIP" when it reads their threads. Deleting or pausing the memory
removes exactly that VIP flag (VIPs you marked by hand in Inbox/Hidden stay).

## Saving a memory from a specific email

When you have an item selected on the dashboard, the right-hand rail's
**Memory** tab shows which of your memories Vesta applied to that item — and
lets you add a new one on the spot. Memories added there are pinned to that
sender (you'll see "Applies to <their email>"), so "always escalate Maya"
affects Maya's threads and nobody else's. Tone and company-context memories
always apply everywhere.

## When Vesta suggests a memory

Vesta never saves anything on its own. When you steer a draft with a custom
instruction and send it, Vesta may **suggest** remembering that instruction as
a preference for that person. Suggestions appear at the top of Memory & Rules
under **"Vesta suggests — waiting for your approval"**:

- **Approve** — it becomes a normal active memory and starts being used.
- **Reject** — it is deleted and never applied.

Until you approve, a suggestion does nothing at all.

## Pause, resume, forget

Every saved memory card has:

- **Pause / Resume** — keep the memory but stop (or restart) using it. Paused
  memories are greyed out and ignored by the AI and the VIP flags.
- **✕ (Forget)** — delete it permanently.

## Where your memories are used

- **Radar ranking & reasons** — the AI reads your VIPs, delegation rules, hard
  limits, and context when it scores each thread, picks its category, and
  writes the "why this matters" you see in the rail.
- **Draft replies** — drafts follow your tone and preferences, never cross a
  "Do NOT do" rule, and can use your saved project/company background instead
  of inventing things.
- **Filtering & priority** — VIP senders skip the noise gate and rank higher.

Memories pinned to a person only fire on that person's threads; everything
else applies to all your mail.

## Privacy

Memory & Rules is yours alone — it is read only when Vesta works on **your**
mailbox, and nothing in it is ever shared or sent anywhere except inside the
AI request that ranks your own email or writes your own draft.
