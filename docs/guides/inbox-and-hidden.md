# Inbox & Hidden mail

Vesta gives you two lists of synced mail: your **Inbox** (real correspondence) and
your **Hidden** list (mail filtered out as noise). Both are explained by the two
filtering gates in [How Vesta filters your email](email-filtering.md) — this guide
covers what you'll see and do in each.

## The Inbox

Open **Inbox** from the sidebar. It shows the real correspondence Vesta synced —
the mail that passed filtering — **grouped by conversation**. A reply to an email
doesn't create a new row; it stays on the same thread, with a small count showing
how many messages it holds. For each thread you'll see the latest sender, subject, a
short preview, and the time **in your own timezone**. Unread threads are marked with
an accent line.

**Click any thread to open the full conversation** — the [thread view](#opening-a-full-email--thread)
shows every message, oldest to newest, with the complete email body.

> The Inbox is broader than your dashboard. It includes everything that's genuine
> correspondence, whether or not it's waiting on your reply. The dashboard shows only
> the items that need *you* — see [Your dashboard & Priorities](priorities-and-dashboard.md).

### Opening a full email / thread — the reading room

Clicking a thread (from the Inbox, the dashboard's "Open full thread", or a
Priorities row) opens the **reading room** — the conversation as Vesta sees it:

- **Vesta's read, pinned on top.** If Vesta has analyzed this thread for your
  radar, you'll see its one-line summary, *why it matters*, the category and
  due date — and a **"Draft a reply with Vesta"** button. The draft lands in
  Draft Replies for your approval; nothing sends without you.
- **A clean timeline, not a wall of text.** Each message sits on a timeline
  with the sender's colored initials. The **newest message is open**; older
  ones are collapsed to one preview line — tap any of them to read in full.
  Your own replies are marked **You**.
- **No more repeated history.** Replies normally re-paste the whole earlier
  conversation under "From: … Sent: …" — Vesta tucks that away behind a
  **"Show quoted history"** toggle, so each card shows only what that person
  actually wrote. The full body (formatting, signatures, images) is rendered
  exactly as sent.

There's an **Open in Outlook** button if you want the native client.

> Full bodies are saved going forward. Emails synced before this feature show their
> body once your mailbox re-syncs them.

### Tuning filtering from the Inbox

Each message has quick actions so you can teach Vesta as you read:

- **Mute** — stop showing this sender (they'll move to Hidden going forward).
- **Mark VIP** — always show this person, and treat their mail as important.

## The Hidden list

This is where Vesta collects mail it filtered out as noise — newsletters, alerts,
automated senders, and anything Outlook sorted into *Other*.

**Where to find it:**
- From the **sidebar** — a **Hidden** item in the Workspace section opens it directly.
- From **Settings → Outlook card → "Review hidden mail →"**.

The Hidden list's back button returns you to the **dashboard**.

Each hidden message shows a short **reason** it was filtered (e.g. *"Automated
sender (no-reply / notifications)"* or *"Outlook sorted this to Other"*), so you're
never guessing.

### Overriding what Vesta hid

If something was hidden that you actually want, one click fixes it permanently:

- **Always allow** — this sender will stop being hidden from now on.
- **Mark VIP** — promote the person to VIP; their mail is always shown and treated
  as important.

> **Tip:** until you trust the filtering, glance at Hidden every few days and
> *Always allow* / *Mark VIP* the people who matter. Your choices make filtering
> increasingly personal and accurate.
